The `surface-to-surface vector` generator defines a vector data field on the domain bounded by two surfaces.

The vectors are generated by first solving a scalar Laplace equation in the domain with different values on the bounded surfaces as boundary conditions. An iterative approach is used to solve this equation and the `smooth_iters` is the max number of iterations used. 

Then, the normalized gradient $\mathbf{q}$ of the solution to the Laplace equation is taken. 

If the `cross` flag is set, the cross product is taken with the `normal` vector that is specified by the user.

\[
    \mathbf{q} \leftarrow \mathbf{q} \times \mathbf{N}
\]

Finally, if the `in_angle` and `out_angle` are given (in degrees), an additional in-plane and out-of-plane rotations are applied, where the plane is defined by the normal vector.

```xml
<MeshData>
  <ElementData name="fiber_map" generator="surface-to-surface vector" elem_set="Part1">
    <surface1 surface="surface1"/>
    <surface2 surface="surface2"/>
    <smooth_iters>1000</smooth_iters>
  <ElementData>
</MeshData>
```

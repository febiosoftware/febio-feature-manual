FEBio’s `solid` module provide a nonlinear finite element framework tailored to biological and soft tissue mechanics. The formulation supports large deformations, geometric and material nonlinearities, and complex constitutive behavior.

Core capabilities include:

* Finite-strain kinematics with updated Lagrangian formulation.
* A wide range of hyperelastic material models (e.g., Neo-Hookean, Mooney–Rivlin, Ogden, Holzapfel-type fiber-reinforced models).
* Anisotropic, transversely isotropic, and orthotropic materials.
* Viscoelasticity, including quasi-linear and fully nonlinear formulations.
* Plasticity and damage models for permanent deformation and material degradation.
* Active contraction models for muscle and other contractile tissues.
* Contact algorithms (sliding, tied, frictional) for large-deformation contact.
* Rigid body mechanics and constraints for multi-body systems.

These capabilities allow FEBio to simulate complex soft tissue mechanics, implant–tissue interactions, musculoskeletal systems, and other nonlinear biomechanical problems.
